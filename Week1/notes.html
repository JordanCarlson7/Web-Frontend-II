Mobile First

Back
  • Gets your core and simple and beautiful features down first
  • Responsive Retrofitting, is not mobile first although it accomplishes the same purpose just less effective
  • Start small, and work your way up
  • UX improvements
  • People don’t know what they want until you show it to them, observe what they do not what they say
  • Give them what they came for quickly, make them think less. Smooth and seamless.
  • Reachability, don’t make it hard
  • Reduce heavy size data, photos and fonts
  • Minify the documents to reduce loading times
  • With software we just “make do”
  • Make the bottom of the app the part that is most accessible by the fingers
  • People use them easier and like them more and think the app itself moves faster
  • Make buttons look like buttons
  • Give the user what they want right off the bat
  • Mobile Inputs (Forms)
  • Mobile forces simplicity, lessen the cognitive load
  • Demand less information so they don’t have to scroll or add unnecessary information
  • Don’t cram info
  • Give the user feedback, let them know how their progress is going
  • Use autofill
  • Just make it easy
  • Make advertising better
  • Parallax ads are good
  • Advertise sparingly, content comes first
  • Don’t inconvenience the user. Nobody really likes ads
